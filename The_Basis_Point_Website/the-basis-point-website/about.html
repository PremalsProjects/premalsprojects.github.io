About The Basis Point

A systematic approach to building market intuition

The Mission

The Basis Point exists to democratize institutional-grade market analysis. Too many resources focus on what happened. We focus on why it happened and how markets reacted.

Every trading day, professional analysts at Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and BlackRock receive comprehensive briefings that explain market mechanics, reaction chains, and sector implications. This site brings that same analytical framework to anyone willing to learn.

Why "The Basis Point"?

A basis point is 1/100th of one percent‚Äîthe smallest unit that moves bond markets and determines trillion-dollar portfolio decisions. It represents precision, attention to detail, and the understanding that small moves compound into massive outcomes.

It's also a play on words: this is the foundational point where your market education begins. One basis point at a time.

Markets don't move in isolation. When CPI falls, it triggers a cascade: Fed expectations shift ‚Üí bond yields drop ‚Üí sector rotation begins ‚Üí currencies react. We map these complete chains so you learn to predict them.

Example: "CPI at 2.4% ‚Üí 83% June cut odds ‚Üí 10Y yield to 4.11% ‚Üí REITs +2.1%, Banks -0.8%"

üìä Four-Driver Framework

Every market move ultimately links back to four macro drivers: Inflation, Interest Rates, Employment, and Growth. By tracking all four daily with institutional data (FRED, CME FedWatch, BLS), you build a complete market picture.

Sources: US CPI, Fed Funds Rate, NFP data, Treasury Yield Curve, PMI releases

üí° Market Mechanics Education

Most analysis tells you "rates are falling" without explaining why that helps utilities but hurts banks. We explain the actual mechanisms: dividend yield competition, net interest margin compression, discount rate effects on DCF models.

Teaching: Not just data, but understanding how professionals think about that data

Each briefing includes homework: predict how ADP will move markets, watch Fed minutes in real-time and note the algorithmic response, track one sector for a week. Active learning builds lasting intuition.
